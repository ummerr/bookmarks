# Flow + Omni — Exec Brief

**Audience:** Flow / Gemini leadership · **Date:** June 3, 2026 · **Status:** Internal / unlisted

**The one line:** Flow won the "video editor" framing and is the best-loved surface for Omni — sentiment is strongly positive and held flat into week 2 — but a missing API, an open over-censorship bug, and only-partially-healed quota trust are capping the win.

**By the numbers** — 132 curated posts (May 19 – Jun 2, 2026), re-pulled from the live bookmark corpus and re-scored in one pass:

- **69% positive · +81 net** on Omni overall — and **75% positive on Flow specifically** (48 posts). Flow is the best-received surface.
- Sentiment **held flat** post-launch (69% → 67% in week 2). No backlash decay.
- **~45% of all posts are editing praise** — the "Nano Banana for video" reframe is now the consensus.

## What's working

- **The editing reframe is the moat — and it stuck.** Launch-day "weaker than Seedance" disappointment flipped within days to "it's not a generator, it's an *editor*." *"On est sur le nano banana de la vidéo… le comparer à seedance était une grossière erreur de jugement"* (@sat0oshi). Google's own product leads leaned into the editing framing publicly on May 29 — and the corpus agrees.
- **Flow is the hero surface.** Flow-named posts run 75% positive vs 69% overall. The new **Character feature** — register a *personality*, not just a look — is landing internationally (JP, ES, AR): *"一貫性の対象が見た目 → 所作 → 人格へと深層化"* / "consistency deepens from appearance → behavior → persona" (@kenichiota0711, May 28).
- **A creator-economy flywheel is forming.** Week-2 posts shifted from "this is magic" to monetization and how-to: *"printing $9,400/month"* (@shmidtqq), *"$14,200 from 15-second videos"* (@Lummox_eth) — all routing through Flow + Omni. Early proof of the distribution thesis.

## What's bleeding

- **Quota trust is only half-healed.** The May 28 adjustment (2× Ultra Omni generations, failed generations no longer charged, Flash-Lite free) honored Pichai's "progress soon" *partially* — but it's a walk-back of an I/O nerf, not a restoration of the old credit pools. The cancel narrative isn't fully dead.
- **The over-censorship bug is still open.** `b/515000564` is unfixed as of today; harmless prompts still get rejected. *"1. Get rejected for looking too much like a celebrity… 6. switch back to Kling"* (@bitcloud, May 28). It's the one wound directly handing creators to a competitor.
- **No API = no proof and no devs.** Still "coming weeks," no date. This single gap blocks blind-vote leaderboard entry (so "trails Seedance" stays unrebutted) **and** keeps the developer surface dark.

## Competitive snapshot

- **vs Seedance 2.0** — they win raw from-scratch generation (still #1 on the boards); we win editing, workflow, and single-pass audio. Different category — and the market now gets it.
- **vs Sora 2** — OpenAI is sunsetting *consumer* Sora, opening the lane; the gap to watch is audio/personality, where Omni's voices still read as a weak point.
- **vs Kling 3.0** — Kling wins credit economics, and our censorship false-positives are actively sending refused users their way.

## The ask (Flow product)

1. **Finish the quota walk-back** — restore real headroom or ship the promised pay-as-you-go top-ups, so the cancel narrative fully dies. Half-fixes keep the grievance alive.
2. **Ship the API window** — even a dated Vertex / AI Studio preview unblocks devs *and* lets Omni enter the leaderboards to rebut "trails Seedance."
3. **Fix `b/515000564`** — the over-censorship false-positives are the single wound directly handing users to Kling.
4. **Press the Flow lead** — Character + editing + creator-monetization is the winning story. Lean distribution and ungate EU personal-video input to widen it.
